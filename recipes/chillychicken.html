Chilly Chicken - Kerala Style

Description

Chilly Chicken Kerala Style features bite-sized chicken pieces that are marinated, deep-fried until crispy, and then tossed in a luscious gravy made from a blend of spices, coconut milk, and other aromatic ingredients.The dish strikes a perfect balance between heat, tanginess, and creaminess, creating a memorable culinary experience. It's often served as an appetizer or as a main course alongside steamed rice or Kerala-style bread like appam or parotta.

Ingredients

For marinating and frying the chicken:

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

For the gravy:

  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the chicken pieces with ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, soy sauce, cornstarch, and salt. Let it marinate for about 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Once hot, fry the marinated chicken pieces until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  3. In another pan, heat coconut oil. Add chopped ginger, garlic, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until aromatic.
  4. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent.
  5. Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Sauté the spices until fragrant, taking care not to burn them.
  6. Pour in the coconut milk and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Allow it to cook for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Adjust the seasoning with salt as needed.
  8. Add the fried chicken pieces to the gravy and toss well until the pieces are coated evenly.
  9. Continue to cook for a few more minutes, allowing the chicken to absorb the flavors of the gravy.
  10. Once the gravy thickens and coats the chicken, remove from heat.
  11. Serve the Chilly Chicken Kerala Style hot as an appetizer or alongside steamed rice or Kerala bread. Garnish with additional curry leaves and sliced green chilies for an extra burst of flavor and visual appeal. Enjoy the rich and spicy goodness of this delightful dish!
